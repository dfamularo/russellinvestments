3. Tax File Number*


By providing my Tax File Number (TFN), I agree for it to be used as outlined in the TFN Collection Statement. Why is it important to give us your Tax File Number?

Under superannuation law, we can collect your Tax File Number (TFN) for lawful purposes. While you don’t have to give us your TFN, doing so means:

  • we’ll be able to accept all types of contributions to your account(s);

  • the tax on contributions to your super account(s) will not increase;

  • other than the tax that may generally apply, no additional tax will be deducted when you start drawing down your super benefits and;

  • it will make it much easier to trace different super accounts in your name, so that you receive all your super benefits when you retire.

The Trustee of your super fund may share your TFN with another super provider when your benefits are being transferred, unless you ask the Trustee in writing that your TFN not be shared with any other super provider. Your TFN will otherwise remain confidential.

Can't find your TFN? Call the Australian Taxation Office on 132 861.

 Automatically rebalance to my future investment strategy. What does this mean?

This means automatically switching investment units between the above options to realign your investment with the strategy you have chosen. This helps you to maintain the risk level that you’re most comfortable with and stay focused on your investment objectives.

Automatic re-balancing will happen on the 15th day of each month, so you won’t be able to switch your investments for the three working days before this date.

6. Consolidate your super*

Send us on a search to find your other super and consolidate it all into your iQ Super – Saver account. Do make sure you read this important information.

Before you consolidate, it’s important to find out if you’ll have to pay any exit fees or charges, if your tax and preservation component will be impacted or if you’ll lose any features and benefits that are important to you, on leaving your other super fund(s).

Also, any insurance cover you have with your other super fund(s) will be cancelled, if you roll your money out and close your account(s) with your other super fund(s). It’s important to think about how this will impact your insurance needs before consolidating your super.
